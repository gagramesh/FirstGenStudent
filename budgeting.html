Why Budgeting Matters

Being smart with your money in college isn’t about cutting out everything fun — it’s about making your money work for you and avoiding unnecessary debt.

Step-by-Step Budget Plan

  1. Calculate your income – Include financial aid refunds, part-time job income, family support, etc.
  2. Track your spending – Use apps like Mint or EveryDollar.
  3. Make a monthly budget – Separate wants vs. needs and set spending limits.
  4. Adjust as needed – Life happens! Your budget should flex with it.

Sample Student Budget

  • Rent: $500
  • Groceries: $150
  • Transportation: $60
  • Phone & Internet: $70
  • Books & Supplies: $100
  • Fun / Eating Out: $50

Other Tips

  • Buy used books or rent them online
  • Use your school’s food pantry if available
  • Track student discounts (Spotify, Apple, restaurants)
  • Split costs with roommates when you can
